Open Arms of Minnesota

The Problem:
Open Arms of Minnesota is a nonprofit that delivers food to individuals in the Minneapolis area who are struggling both financially and physically. With the COVID-19 pandemic they are seeing a huge jump in client numbers, and need a more accessible site and succinct sign-up process to increase volunteer turnout. 

The Solution:
Redesign the site with the intent of increasing volunteer sign-up. 

My Role:
UI Designer (Group Project)

Tools:
Adobe XD, Figma, Miro, Invision, G Suite

Timeline:
2 weeks

Problem Statement

“The Open Arms of Minnesota website was designed to acquire and inform volunteers that support our purpose - to feed individuals who are unable to feed themselves due to illness and/or financial hardship. We have observed that our product or service isn’t meeting these goals, which is causing fewer volunteers to register and fewer individuals to be served. How might we improve the Open Arms of Minnesota website so that our customers are successful based on an increase in the number of volunteers and individuals served?"

03. Ideation


"I Like, I Wish, What If" and Feature Prioritization Matrix

In a group, a diversity of “I like”, “I wish”, and “what if” statements were used to brainstorm features. These ideas were then organized by impact and effort. Low effort, high impact ideas were prioritized.

“I like large buttons!”
“I like easy sign up pages.”
“I wish I could sign up for volunteering in five steps or less.”
“I wish there was a simple animation that showed what the organization did.”
“What if there was a way to sign up the day I wanted to volunteer.”

06. Conclusions & Future Opportunities


Conclusions


During the reserach phase we were able to determine that the most likely Users of the site to be prospective Volunteers. The current sign up process takes you off the site and is in the form of a long daunting print out. With numbers up in response to COVID we knew we needed to focus our energy on streamlining the Volunteer sign up process. We were able to create a 5-step sign up form and enabled volunteers to register without leaving the site.
